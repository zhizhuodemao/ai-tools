# Discovery

Use this reference to build the resume profile, target profile, multi-direction plan, 30-candidate scan, and calibration report.

## Resume Profile

Extract only job-search evidence:

- current or recent role,
- years and seniority signals,
- city, remote, salary, availability clues,
- core skills with resume evidence,
- project evidence and measurable outcomes,
- target role signals,
- weak or missing evidence,
- truthful greeting strategy angles,
- hard constraints and exclusions.

Do not invent skills, years, credentials, salaries, or project facts.

## Multi-Direction Plan

Generate several directions from the resume and user constraints. For reverse/security/AI resumes, typical probes may include:

- Android or APP reverse,
- mobile security or risk-control adversarial work,
- JS or Web reverse,
- security research or vulnerability/adversarial roles,
- AI engineering plus data collection or automation.

Use at least 5 keyword or direction probes when the market allows. Do not collect all 30 candidates from one keyword or one direction. If the market is narrow, report why.

## Valid Candidate

A valid candidate requires all of these:

- discovered from visible BOSS UI,
- opened through a visible click,
- job detail read,
- company-page visit attempted through visible UI,
- company facts recorded,
- company posted-job pattern recorded when visible,
- fixed scoring completed.

List-page-only items do not count.

## 30-Candidate Scan

For the first serious market scan, inspect at least 30 valid candidates when browser state and market supply allow it.

If fewer than 30 are possible, record:

- directions tried,
- candidates per direction,
- blockers,
- market scarcity or quality collapse,
- why automation should or should not proceed from the smaller sample.

Do not enable automation from a small sample unless the user explicitly accepts the risk.

## Calibration Report

After the 30-candidate scan, stop before any account-changing action. Produce `reports/latest.md` with:

- resume profile summary,
- directions tried and candidate counts,
- market pattern by direction,
- strongest fits,
- rejected patterns,
- company-page coverage and blockers,
- salary and city reality,
- recommended target profile,
- proposed automation mode and limit options,
- concise next-step choices.

Ask the user to refine or confirm the target profile, automation mode, and daily limits before handoff. Do not convert proposed limits into policy until the user explicitly chooses or confirms them.
